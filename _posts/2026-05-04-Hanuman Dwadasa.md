# Shri Hanuman Dwadasa Nama Stotram
## (The 12 Names of Lord Hanuman)

A powerful hymn invoked for protection, courage, and victory in all endeavors.

---

### 1. The Twelve Names
**Verses 1 & 2**
*hanumānañjanīsūnuḥ vāyuputro mahābalaḥ |*
*rāmēṣṭaḥ phālgunasakhaḥ piṅgākṣo'mitavikramaḥ || 1 ||*
*udadhikramaṇaścaiva sītāśokavināśanaḥ |*
*lakṣmaṇaprāṇadātā ca daśagrīvasya darpahā || 2 ||*

| No. | Name | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Hanumān** | He with the prominent jaw |
| 2 | **Añjanīsūnu** | Son of Anjana |
| 3 | **Vāyuputra** | Son of the Wind God |
| 4 | **Mahābala** | One with immense strength |
| 5 | **Rāmēṣṭa** | Beloved of Lord Rama |
| 6 | **Phālgunasakha** | Friend of Arjuna (Phalguna) |
| 7 | **Piṅgākṣa** | The reddish/golden-eyed one |
| 8 | **Amitavikrama** | He of boundless valor |
| 9 | **Udadhikramaṇa** | He who crossed the ocean |
| 10 | **Sītāśokavināśana** | Destroyer of Sita's grief |
| 11 | **Lakṣmaṇaprāṇadātā** | Giver of life to Lakshmana |
| 12 | **Daśagrīvasya Darpahā** | Destroyer of Ravana's pride |

---

### 2. When to Recite
**Verse 3**
*ēvaṁ dvādaśanāmāni kapīndrasya mahātmanaḥ |*
*svāpakālē prabodhē ca yātrākālē ca yaḥ paṭhēt || 3 ||*

Recite these twelve names of the great-souled King of Monkeys:
* **Svāpakālē:** At the time of sleep.
* **Prabodhē:** Upon waking up.
* **Yātrākālē:** Before starting a journey.

---

### 3. The Benefit (Phalashruti)
*tasya sarvabhayaṁ nāsti sarvatra vijayī bhavēt ||*

> **Translation:** For that person, all fear ceases to exist, and they shall emerge victorious in everything they do.
>

### Dhana Sholka from Valmiki Ramanyam -  I bow with folded hands to that Āñjaneya (Hanumān)—Who, playfully leaping across the waters of the ocean, took upon Himself the ‘fire of sorrow’ of Janakātmājā (Sītā), and with that very fire burned down Laṅkā.

*ullaṅghya sindhoḥ salilaṁ salīlaṁ* 
*yaḥ śokavahniṁ janakātmajāyāḥ |* 
*ādāya tēnaiva dadāha laṅkāṁ *
*namāmi taṁ prāñjalir āñjanēyam ||*
