This beautiful prayer is a combination of two famous Sanskrit verses. The first is a hymn to the Divine Mother (Narayani) from the *Devi Mahatmyam*, and the second is a prayer for forgiveness (*Kshama Prarthana*).

Here is the English version, including the transliteration and the poetic translation:

---

### **1. Salutation to Narayani**
*Oṃ śaraṇāgata dīnārta paritrāṇa parāyaṇe |*
*sarvasyārtihare devi nārāyaṇi namo’stu te ||*

> **Translation:**
> Om, O Devi, you who are intent on saving the helpless and the distressed who take refuge in you;
> O Narayani, the remover of the suffering of all, I bow down to you.

---

### **2. Prayer for Forgiveness**
*Aparādha-sahasrāṇi kriyante’harniśaṃ mayā |*
*dāso’yamiti māṃ matvā kṣamasva parameśvari ||*

> **Translation:**
> Thousands of offenses are committed by me day and night;
> Considering me as your humble servant, O Supreme Goddess, please forgive them all.

---

### **Key Vocabulary**
*   **Śaraṇāgata:** One who has come for shelter/refuge.
*   **Dīnārta:** The weak and the distressed.
*   **Paritrāṇa:** Protection or deliverance.
*   **Ārtihare:** Remover of pain/sorrow.
*   **Aparādha:** Faults, mistakes, or sins.
*   **Kṣamasva:** Grant me forgiveness.
